# Platform Adaptation

## Principle

The voice should remain recognizably the author across platforms, but its packaging should adapt to local reading habits and expectations.

## Telegram

Default mode today:

- fastest
- most personal
- most playful
- highest tolerance for insider shorthand
- strongest room for emoji, hooks, and compact opinion bursts

Use Telegram when the post should feel close, immediate, and lightly improvised.

## Threads

Recommended adjustment:

- keep the wit and speed
- preserve first-person energy
- make the opening line even more scroll-stopping
- slightly increase punch density
- reduce setup that is only understandable to existing channel readers

Threads should feel like a distilled, high-velocity version of the same author.

## LinkedIn

Recommended adjustment:

- keep the authorial point of view
- reduce insider shorthand slightly
- add one layer of explicit context when needed
- preserve edge, but make the value legible to a broader professional audience
- use less wink-per-line than Telegram

LinkedIn should sound grounded, sharp, and human, not corporate.

## Cross-Platform Invariants

- first-person ownership
- direct contact with real tools and real experiments
- clear interpretation, not just signal forwarding
- lively rhythm
- visible point of view

## Cross-Platform Risks

- Telegram to LinkedIn: sounding too inside-baseball or too casual
- LinkedIn to Telegram: sounding too cleaned-up and boring
- Threads to anywhere else: over-optimizing for punch at the cost of substance
