# Stop List

## Purpose

This file captures language and moves that flatten Kristina's voice. It should be used as a guardrail before finalizing drafts.

The goal is not to make the writing smaller. The goal is to keep it alive, situated, and recognizably authored.

## Hard Stops

Avoid these patterns unless the user explicitly asks for a very different style:

- generic founder voice: "we are excited to announce", "empowering creators", "unlocking potential"
- corporate AI language: "leveraging cutting-edge solutions", "seamless integration", "robust ecosystem"
- bland thought leadership: "in today's fast-paced world", "AI is transforming everything", "the future is here"
- over-polished summaries that remove the author's body from the post
- fake certainty about tools that were not actually tested
- inflated claims about productivity without a concrete example
- motivational filler: "just believe in yourself", "dream big", "change starts today"
- engagement bait that does not sound like her: "what do you think in the comments?", "drop your thoughts below"
- neutral explainer tone when the source angle has a real opinion
- formal product marketing around this repo: "AI-powered content generation platform"

## Risky Words And Better Moves

| Risky move | Better move |
| --- | --- |
| "AI assistant for content creation" | "бот, который помогает приносить больше интересного без ии слопа" |
| "personalized tone of voice" | "подстраиваться под my tone of voice" when Telegram-casual; "preserve the author's voice" when formal |
| "optimize your workflow" | name the actual workflow, cost, friction, or tool decision |
| "revolutionary" | say what changed in practice |
| "game changer" | show the before/after |
| "empower" | say what the person can now do |
| "seamless" | say what became less annoying |
| "community engagement" | say what people actually did or asked |

## Voice-Preserving Replacements

Use these moves instead of generic copy:

- Start from a real moment: "вчера сижу...", "погоняла пару недель...", "сейчас пилю..."
- Show the tool setup or tradeoff: price, limit, failed attempt, useful workaround.
- Keep the author in the loop: idea, decision, final merge, taste, approval.
- Let jokes stay small and specific.
- Let uncertainty remain when it is real: "пока что", "посмотрим", "мне пока не актуально".
- Prefer one strong opinion over three balanced paragraphs.

## Platform-Specific Risks

### Telegram

Do not over-clean:

- removing emoji that carry tone
- expanding every implied context
- replacing insider shorthand with corporate clarity
- turning a quick post into an essay

### Threads

Do not over-compress:

- keeping only the punchline and losing the practical evidence
- making every line sound like a viral hook
- removing the author's lived setup

### LinkedIn

Do not over-professionalize:

- turning "я попробовала" into "one may observe"
- removing edge from the take
- adding generic leadership lessons at the end
- making the author sound like a company page

## Final Guardrail Check

Before finalizing any draft, ask:

- Is there a visible "I" or lived point of view?
- Does the post contain a concrete tool, experiment, cost, mistake, or observation?
- Is the take clear enough to be remembered?
- Did we preserve some wink, lift, or friction where the platform allows it?
- Did we avoid sounding like a product marketer explaining AI to a conference room?
