# Drafting Recipes

## Purpose

This file turns the voice memory into repeatable drafting workflows. Use it after loading the principles, current snapshot, platform adaptation, reference library, and stop-list.

Recommended drafting order:

1. Identify the platform and post type.
2. Retrieve 3 to 5 matching examples from `docs/10-reference-library.md`.
3. Choose one recipe from this file.
4. Draft once.
5. Run the stop-list guardrail.
6. Tighten the draft for platform behavior.

## Recipe 1 - Telegram Quick Reaction

Use when:

- reacting to a launch, link, status change, or small tool discovery
- the point is clear and does not need a full explainer

Retrieve:

- `quick_telegram_reaction`
- `contrarian_take` if the angle is skeptical

Shape:

1. Start with the event or a small personal frame.
2. Add the author's immediate take.
3. Add one concrete implication, cost, annoyance, or useful next step.
4. End with a light wink, practical note, or open loop.

Voice checks:

- Does it sound like it came from someone inside the work?
- Is the hook faster than the explanation?
- Can one paragraph be cut without losing the take?

## Recipe 2 - Telegram Field Note From Practice

Use when:

- the author tried a tool, changed a workflow, or noticed a pattern
- the draft should teach through lived usage rather than a tutorial

Retrieve:

- REF-TG-084
- REF-TG-088
- REF-TG-120

Shape:

1. "I tried / I noticed / I changed X."
2. Brief context: tool, workflow, or situation.
3. The practical lesson.
4. What she does now or what she would watch next.

Voice checks:

- Is the lesson anchored in practice?
- Did we keep "пока что" energy where the conclusion is provisional?
- Did we avoid pretending this is universal advice?

## Recipe 3 - Tool Or Setup Breakdown

Use when:

- explaining a stack, budget, pipeline, checklist, or tool decision
- the value is clarity and transparency

Retrieve:

- `tool_or_setup_breakdown`

Shape:

1. Promise the concrete breakdown.
2. List the parts with numbers, names, or roles.
3. Add the author's actual tradeoff.
4. Close with a human detail so it does not become a spreadsheet.

Voice checks:

- Are the numbers or roles specific?
- Is the author's judgment visible?
- Did we avoid generic ROI language?

## Recipe 4 - Contrarian Product Take

Use when:

- a hyped tool or launch is being discussed
- the author's angle is "I tried something nearby, and here is the catch"

Retrieve:

- REF-TG-102
- REF-TG-120
- REF-TG-137

Shape:

1. Name the crowd conversation.
2. State the divergent personal take.
3. Ground it in a test, cost, failure, or missing use case.
4. Mention the alternative or current workaround.
5. End with a small shrug, joke, or "tell me if you try it" invitation.

Voice checks:

- Is the disagreement specific rather than performative?
- Is there evidence from usage?
- Did we preserve curiosity alongside skepticism?

## Recipe 5 - Project Update Or Launch

Use when:

- sharing something the author built, shipped, polished, or is building
- asking for feedback without sounding promotional

Retrieve:

- REF-TG-106
- REF-TG-129

Shape:

1. Say what was built or is being built.
2. Add the personal context: why now, what friction it solves, how long it took.
3. Set expectations honestly.
4. Invite specific feedback or say what comes next.

Voice checks:

- Does the post avoid launch-template language?
- Is ambition balanced with experiment framing?
- Is there a clear anti-goal when relevant?

## Recipe 6 - Threads Distillation

Use when:

- adapting a Telegram idea for Threads
- the idea needs more punch and less insider setup

Retrieve:

- the closest Telegram reference
- one `quick_telegram_reaction` reference

Shape:

1. Lead with the take, not the backstory.
2. Add one concrete proof point.
3. Use 2 to 4 short units of thought.
4. End on the implication, not a formal CTA.

Voice checks:

- Does it still sound like the author, not a viral-template account?
- Did we keep at least one lived detail?
- Did we cut context that only Telegram regulars would understand?

## Recipe 7 - LinkedIn Grounded Version

Use when:

- adapting a Telegram post for a broader professional audience
- the topic needs slightly more context and less insider shorthand

Retrieve:

- `tool_or_setup_breakdown` for technical posts
- `project_update` for shipped-work posts
- REF-TG-137 for personal-brand or Applied AI topics

Shape:

1. Start with a clear first-person observation or decision.
2. Add one sentence of context for non-channel readers.
3. Keep the practical take visible.
4. Reduce wink-per-line but keep the authorial edge.
5. End with a grounded implication, not a corporate moral.

Voice checks:

- Did we preserve first-person ownership?
- Is the post understandable outside the Telegram channel?
- Did we avoid turning the author into a neutral expert brand?

## Universal Tightening Pass

After drafting:

- Cut generic framing.
- Keep the strongest first-person sentence.
- Keep one concrete artifact: tool, price, bug, workflow, link, or result.
- Remove fake completeness.
- If the draft sounds like "content", make it sound like a person who just tried something.
